The real problem

The quote looked good until real fabrication showed up.

A lot of fabrication shops are not losing money because the crew is lazy or the shop cannot build. They are losing money because the quote did not capture the full job.

The estimate caught the obvious steel, but missed the hidden labour. It carried the vendor quote, but missed the freight and coordination. It added markup, but did not create the margin the owner thought was there.

! The shop runs out of hours on jobs that looked profitable on bid day.
! The quote does not clearly define assumptions, exclusions, install conditions, or change-order rules.
! The business stays busy, but the profit disappears after award.

What you will learn

The five quote checks covered in the free training.

These are practical checks you can use before you send a number, especially when drawings are messy, field work is unclear, labour is uncertain, or the customer is pushing for a fast price.

Check 01

Bad Quote Autopsy

Learn how to look back at a job that lost money and identify where the quote failed. Labour, material, scope, install, outsourcing, risk, or weak quote language.

Check 02

True Labour Rate

Learn why wage rate is not labour cost, and why a billable shop hour needs to recover burden, overhead, non-billable time, equipment, admin, and profit.

Check 03

Markup vs Margin

See why a 20% markup is not a 20% margin, and how that quiet mistake can drain thousands from larger fabrication jobs.

Check 04

Scope Gap Checklist

Learn how to catch hidden work around the steel, including handling, setup, finishing, delivery, install access, field measuring, and second mobilization.

Check 05

Quote Confidence Score

Learn a simple way to test your quote for labour, scope, risk, assumptions, exclusions, schedule, terms, and change-order protection before it goes out.
