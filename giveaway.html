The quiet profit leak

Your shop can be busy and still be undercharging every hour.

If your rate is based on what sounds normal in the market, or what you used last year, it may not be recovering the real cost of labour. Wages are only the start. Paid time off, payroll burden, non-billable time, overhead, shop support, taxes, and profit all need a home.

The Labor Rate Calculator helps you turn those costs into a number you can actually quote with, then gives you a simple job quote tab so you can use that rate immediately.

The estimating trap

Arc time is not shop time.

A weld might only take a few minutes to run. But the job still includes setup, print review, staging, clamping, flipping, checking, grinding, cleaning, finish protection, loading, field travel, site friction, and all the small actions that never show up in the arc calculation.

The Arc-to-Floor Ratio Calculator helps convert visible weld time into real floor time so the hidden labour does not quietly come out of your margin.

Questions

Before you download.

Are these actual spreadsheet files?

Yes. The download is built around Excel workbook files for fabrication estimating. They include guided inputs, formulas, calculated outputs, and plain-English explanations.

Is this only for shop owners?

No. The tools are useful for shop owners, estimators, foremen, project managers, welders moving into quoting, and anyone responsible for pricing fabrication work.

What is the AFR Calculator?

AFR means Arc-to-Floor Ratio. It helps compare arc-only weld time against the real labour hours created by setup, handling, access, position, repeat work, fatigue, field work, and risk.

What should I use first?

Start with the Labor Rate Calculator so you know what your hour needs to sell for. Then use the AFR Calculator to check whether your labour hours are too clean.

Will this replace estimating judgment?

No. It gives your judgment a stronger structure. You still need to know the work, read the drawings, ask questions, and apply shop experience.

Why are you giving these away?

Because once you see how much labour and overhead can hide inside a bad quote, the need for a better estimating system becomes obvious.
